Master Thesis Student

SoftPro Medical Solutions is a specialized healthcare technology company that develops innovative software solutions for medical equipment management and quality assurance, focusing on helping healthcare institutions optimize their operational workflows while maintaining compliance with stringent medical industry standards.

During my master's studies, I had the unique opportunity to bridge academic research with real-world healthcare technology challenges through a collaborative thesis project with SoftPro Medical Solutions, addressing critical quality assurance needs in one of Stockholm's major hospital systems.

Research Collaboration and Problem Definition: The thesis project emerged from a genuine industry need to enhance the Quality Assurance system at Södersjukhuset's radiology department. Working closely with SoftPro Medical Solutions, I focused on integrating their proprietary inventory management system, Medusa, with existing radiology equipment service workflows. This collaboration provided invaluable insight into how academic research can directly address practical healthcare operational challenges.

Scientific Methodology and Project Management: Applied rigorous scientific methodology to tackle complex integration challenges while developing essential project management skills in a professional healthcare technology environment. The project required balancing academic research standards with industry timelines and practical implementation constraints, providing experience in managing stakeholder expectations across academic and commercial contexts.

Digital Transformation and Compliance: Led the initiative to centralize documentation storage for radiology equipment quality checks within a comprehensive digital database system. This transformation addressed the critical need for organized, accessible documentation while ensuring compliance with modern medical industry standards, including ISO 13485 requirements and regulations established by Strålskyddsmyndigheten (the Swedish Radiation Safety Authority). The work required deep understanding of both technical implementation requirements and regulatory compliance frameworks.

Workflow Optimization and Efficiency Analysis: Conducted systematic analysis of existing quality assurance workflows to identify redundancies and inefficiencies that impacted operational performance. Through detailed process mapping and stakeholder interviews, I developed recommendations for workflow improvements that would enhance both efficiency and compliance, demonstrating how technology integration can streamline complex healthcare operations.

Industry Integration and Knowledge Transfer: The remote work arrangement during this period required developing strong communication and collaboration skills while working across organizational boundaries. The project successfully demonstrated how academic research could generate practical solutions for healthcare technology challenges, creating value for both the academic institution and industry partner while contributing to improved patient care through enhanced equipment quality assurance processes.
